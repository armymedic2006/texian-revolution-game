# Factions, Units, and Weapons

## Texian Faction Identity

Texians are outnumbered but resilient.

Core advantages:

- Better morale
- Better rifle range
- Higher accuracy for many units
- Strong defensive resilience
- Strong melee persistence
- Powerful named officers
- Special 4x morale/combat boost
- Reduced fatigue penalties

## Texian Unit Types

Version 1 Texian units:

- Texian Regular Army
- Texian Militia
- Tennessee Riflemen
- Artillery Crew
- Named Officers
- Tejano Volunteers

### Texian Regular Army

Traits:

- Better morale than militia
- Better accuracy than militia
- Greater close-range effectiveness than Texian Militia

Default loadout:

- Rifle
- Bowie knife
- One pistol

### Texian Militia

Traits:

- More numerous than Regular Army
- Weaker morale than Regular Army
- Weaker accuracy than Regular Army at close/normal range
- Better performance at longer effective firing range

Default loadout:

- Rifle
- Bowie knife
- One pistol

### Tennessee Riflemen

Tennessee Riflemen are distinct from other Texian units.

Bonuses:

- 1.4x accuracy multiplier above other rifled units
- 3x melee multiplier

Default loadout:

- Rifle
- Bowie knife
- One pistol

### Tejano Volunteers

Bonuses:

- Better stealth / fog-of-war spotting
- Better morale near Juan Seguín
- +15% morale over all Texian units except Tennessee Riflemen

Default loadout:

- Rifle
- Bowie knife
- One pistol

Available in both Historical and Alternate-History roster modes.

### Texian Artillery Crew

Artillery crew operates Texian artillery.

Crew can:

- Operate cannon
- Fire personal weapons
- Count for victory point control
- Move eligible artillery if the cannon is movable

### Texian Named Officers

Named officers are covered in `docs/05-officers.md`.

## Mexican Faction Identity

Mexican strength comes from:

- Larger numbers
- Officers
- Artillery accuracy
- Coordinated assaults
- Close-range musket effectiveness
- Cavalry
- Sappers and assault works

## Mexican Unit Types

Version 1 Mexican units:

- Line Infantry
- Skirmishers / Light Infantry
- Grenadiers
- Artillery Crew
- Named Officers
- Sappers / Breaching Troops
- Cavalry

### Mexican Line Infantry

Baseline Mexican squad type.

Loadout:

- Musket

Special rule:

- +33% combat effectiveness at close/point-blank range

No other special traits beyond baseline musket rule.

### Mexican Skirmishers / Light Infantry

Traits:

- Rifle-armed
- Better range accuracy
- Better movement
- Better spotting/fog-of-war detection
- Significantly weaker melee
- 10% of Line Infantry soldier count
- Can climb, breach, and build simple works, but slower than normal infantry

### Mexican Grenadiers

Traits:

- Better melee only
- No special morale bonus
- 70% of Line Infantry soldier count

### Mexican Sappers / Breaching Troops

Traits:

- Distinct Mexican unit type
- Faster breaching
- Faster assault-work construction
- Better at damaging gates, doors, barricades, and walls
- Better at placing ladders
- Worse ranged combat than Line Infantry
- Worse melee than Line Infantry
- 10% of Line Infantry soldier count

Loadout:

- Musket
- Breaching tools

### Mexican Cavalry

Cavalry is playable in version 1.

Rules:

- Fights only from horseback
- Can enter exterior/open areas
- Can enter courtyards and open interior spaces if pathing allows
- Cannot enter buildings, narrow passages, or tight spaces
- Terrain size and obstacle clearance determine mounted pathing

Bonuses:

- Increased movement speed
- Charge bonus
- Stronger melee when charging
- Initial morale shock if melee results from cavalry charge
- Vulnerable to defensive fire, obstacles, and prepared positions

Confined-area penalties:

- Combat effectiveness reduced by 75%
- Morale reduced by 30%

### Mexican Artillery Crew

Crew can:

- Operate cannon
- Fire personal weapons
- Count for victory point control
- Move eligible artillery if the cannon is movable
