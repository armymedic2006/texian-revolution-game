# Movement, Melee, and Friendly Fire

## Movement

Units move freely within a maximum movement radius.

Movement radius depends on:

- Unit type
- Combat effectiveness
- Morale
- Fatigue
- Formation
- Obstacles
- Defensive positions
- Terrain
- Other units

Units cannot move through friendly or enemy units.

Pathing must route around blocking units.

## Nearby Radius

Morale, leadership, and 4:1 outnumbering checks use:

- Same fixed radius for all units
- Historically meaningful distance in yards/meters
- Must be close enough that melee could happen quickly
- Must also require line of sight

Codex may tune exact value.

## Melee Entry

Offensive melee:

- Requires Charge / Engage Melee order.

Defensive melee:

- If charged while planning ranged attack:
  - Defender fires first if eligible.
  - Then enters melee.
- If charged while not planning ranged attack:
  - Defender automatically enters defensive melee.

Melee continues while units remain within melee range.

Units remain locked only while they remain in melee range.

## Disengagement

All units generally must pass morale check to disengage.

Texian units:

- May disengage only if not Broken or Routed.
- Broken/Routed Texians cannot disengage on their own.
- They remain unless the Mexican unit disengages, routes, retreats, or is destroyed.

Mexican units:

- If Broken/Routed, automatically disengage.
- Then follow Mexican retreat/fallback rules.

## Ranged Weapons in Melee

- Officers may always use melee weapons and pistols.
- First turn of melee allows defensive fire if previously ordered/eligible.
- Later melee turns are melee only for normal units.

## Firing Into Melee

Allowed, including artillery.

Friendly-fire risk depends on angle and positioning.

- Firing from behind friendly unit: high friendly-fire chance
- Firing from flank/side/rear of enemy: higher enemy-hit chance
- Artillery uses same principle but with greater danger

## Friendly Fire

Friendly fire is possible whenever the line of fire directly crosses friendly units.

Applies to:

- Normal ranged attacks
- Artillery
- Firing into melee
- Crowded battlefield situations
